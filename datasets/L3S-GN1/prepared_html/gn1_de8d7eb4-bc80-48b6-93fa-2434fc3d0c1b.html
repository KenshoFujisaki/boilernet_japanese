By David Gauthier-Villars

PARIS -- Clashes between youths and riot police in a suburb north of Paris, a reminder of rampant street violence that hit France two years ago, are rekindling a debate over how to integrate the country's increasingly marginalized immigrant population.

On Wednesday, inhabitants of Villiers-le-Bel -- which is halfway between Paris and Charles de Gaulle International Airport -- awoke yet again to a smashed-up neighborhood. Bands of young people set cars on fire in the suburb for a third night Tuesday, but officials said the violence was less intense than the two previous nights.
