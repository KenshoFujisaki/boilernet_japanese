Mirror, Mirror

Biting and witty fairytale satire takes to the Baxter Sanlam Studio stage

Mike van Graan?s biting and witty fairy-tale satire which contemplates the role of the artist in pre- and post-apartheid South Africa, Mirror, Mirror, takes to the Baxter Sanlam Studio stage from January 8 to February 9 at 8:15pm nightly.

Van Graan has become widely accepted as one of South Africa?s celebrated playwrights. His Bafana Republic won the Best One-Person Show category at the 2007 South African Comedy Awards.

Set in a ?Wizard of Id?-type land with queens, knights and, of course, revolting peasants, Mirror, Mirror is a roller-coaster ride of sharp insights and belly-aching laughter, as the passionate Artymir - a Pieter-Dirk Uys type character - tip-toes through the South African minefield.

Although set in a different era, the parallels with pre-1994 and contemporary South Africa are clear, with the fairytale form and satire being engaging - and unthreatening - vehicles for social commentary.

The multiple award-winning Geoffrey Hyland directs a dynamic young cast of eight highly talented recent graduates from UCT?s Drama Department. Emily Child, Pakamisa Zwedala, Peggy Tunyiswa, Dylan Esbach, Sibongile Balfour, Andrew Laubscher, Portia Mntuyedwa and Tamarin McGinley make up the ensemble.

Mirror, Mirror was UCT's official student entry at the National Arts Festival in 2007 where it played to critical acclaim and enthusiastic audiences on the Fringe and at the subsequent Schools Festival. Sam McCann, coordinator of the Schools Festival confirmed that the show was a real hit among the young delegates. ?I?ve been reading through some of the responses and it looks like they absolutely loved it,? she said. It also played to full houses and standing ovations at the Hilton Arts Festival in September.



This is a play that the whole family will relate to and the post-apartheid generation of young people in particular will find much to enthral them. This season at the Baxter Sanlam Studio also presents audiences with the opportunity to see future South African stars in action and to support young, hugely talented graduates as they enter the challenging world of professional performance.

?With this piece?, says writer, Mike van Graan, ?I wanted to challenge the actors to think about their role as artists in society especially as they were due to graduate later in the year. Even now we have major challenges that art needs to respond to, but at the same time, I wanted the actors to have fun, to show off their training and skill and to seduce audiences with the generosity of their energy, passion and versatility.?

The Herald said, "?ensemble acting like this needs uniformity which this young cast possesses - along with good timing and an abundance of energy to make it a thoroughly enjoyable outing" and went on to call it "a slick brand of sharp humour and piercing introspection... clever.?

Mirror, Mirror runs in the Baxter Sanlam Studio until Saturday, February 9, at 8.15pm nightly, with dinner/show packages available at R120 from Mondays to Thursdays.
