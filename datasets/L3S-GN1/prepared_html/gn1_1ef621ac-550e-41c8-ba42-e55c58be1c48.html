Vardan loses in first round of Chennai Open

Chennai, Dec 31 - National grasscourt champion and wild card entry Vishnu Vardhan lost 1-6, 2-6 against Edouard Roger-Vasselin of France in a first round match in the $436,000 ATP Chennai Open tennis tournament here Monday.

Vardhan was hardly in the frame as the 24-year old Frenchman, ranked 97 in the world, took a mere 51 minutes to race through the two sets.

In another first round match, Florent Serra of France saw off a late challenge from Ivan Navarro (Spain) to win 6-4, 7-6 (6).

Meanwhile, the Spanish pair of Rafael Nadal and Bartolome Salva-Vidal, finalists here last year, lost their first round doubles tie to Marcos Baghdatis (Cyprus) and Marc Gicquel (France) 4-6, 4-6.

Baghdatis, second seed in the singles competition, summed up the performance saying: 'We served well today (Monday) and it feels good to make a winning start. Of course, the doubles helps me to get into my rhythm as I feel the singles draw is extremely tough. So it helps me playing doubles. We held serve through both the sets.'
