Portland crushes Philadelphia to win 13th in a row

Reuters

Published: Sunday, December 30, 2007

NEW YORK (Reuters) - The Portland Trail Blazers rolled to their 13th consecutive victory on Sunday with a crushing 97-72 win over the Philadelphia 76ers.

The Trail Blazers, who have not lost since December 2, got 22 points from Brandon Roy and went on a 21-0 run in the third and fourth quarters to overtake the 76ers. Portland outscored Philadelphia 35-9 in the fourth.

James Jones and Travis Outlaw added 12 points each for Portland (18-12).

Andre Iguodala led the 76ers with 24 points while Andre Miller added 17 points and 11 assists.

Philadelphia led 63-59 with 1:25 to go in the third but Jones hit three successive free throws to pull Portland within one at the end of the quarter.

The red-hot Trail Blazers took the lead for good on Outlaw's jumper to start the fourth quarter and cruised the rest of the way.

*Ben Gordon scored 25 points as the Chicago Bulls beat the New York Knicks 100-83.
