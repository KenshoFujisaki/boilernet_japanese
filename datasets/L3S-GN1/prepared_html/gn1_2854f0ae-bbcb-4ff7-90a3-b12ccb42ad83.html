Dyson ties steals record as Connecticut beats St. John's 81-65

The Associated Press
Published: January 9, 2008
E-Mail Article
Listen to Article
Printer-Friendly
3-Column Format
Translate
Share Article
Text Size

STORRS, Conn. : A.J. Price scored a career-high 25 points and Jerome Dyson tied a team record with nine steals as Connecticut beat St. John's 81-65 Tuesday in the Huskies' Big East home opener.

Jeff Adrien had 20 points and 14 rebounds, and Stanley Robinson added 15 points and eight boards for Connecticut (11-3, 2-1 Big East). Dyson finished with 12 points.

Price hit all four of his 3-point shots, dished out seven assists and grabbed four rebounds.

Freshman Justin Burrell led St. John's (7-7, 1-2) with 17 points.

Connecticut took an early 14-7 lead, turning two blocks from Robinson and two steals from Dyson into easy buckets.

Anthony Mason Jr. scored the first seven for St. John's and finished with 14. Larry Wright added 11, nine of those in the second half.

Dyson made it 22-12 after his fifth steal of the half led to a layup and a foul on Dele Coker. He missed the free throw, but stole the ball back and dunked. That was part of a 14-2 UConn run.

Burrell scored seven consecutive points late in the half to keep the Red Storm in the game, but the Huskies led 45-32 at the break.

A no-look ally-oop pass from Price to Robinson on the fast break, gave the Huskies a 20-point lead, 58-38 five minutes into the second half, bringing the crowd to its feet.

Less than a minute later, Dyson stepped in front of a St. John's pass to tie the steals record which former Husky Scott Burrell (no relation to Justin) set against Maine in 1990. Dyson had eight steals at halftime.

The Huskies have beaten St. John's seven straight times and are 9-0 in conference home openers at Gampel Pavilion.

UConn's 7-foot-3 center Hasheem Thabeet finished with eight points and three of the team's 10 blocked shots.

Connecticut coach Jim Calhoun left the bench in the second half and did not return. Team officials said Calhoun, who had a recent death in his extended family, was suffering from stress and exhaustion.

He is expected to return to the bench when UConn visits No. 7 Georgetown on Saturday.
