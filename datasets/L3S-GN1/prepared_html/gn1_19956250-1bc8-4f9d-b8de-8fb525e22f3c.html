    Gale to drive Nationwide

    After driving three circuits last season, Mobilian to focus on former Busch Series
    Friday, January 18, 2008
    By MARK INABINETT
    Sports Reporter

    Cale Gale is going Nationwide in 2008, starting at Daytona International Speedway on Sunday.

    After driving on three racing circuits last year for Kevin Harvick Inc., the 22-year-old Mobilian will concentrate on the NASCAR Nationwide Series this season. The Nationwide Series is the new name for NASCAR's No. 2 tour, which was known as the Busch Series for the previous 25 years.

    Last year, Gale was at the wheel for KHI in seven Busch, five NASCAR Craftsman Truck and three ARCA RE/MAX Series events. This year, his Nationwide schedule calls for him to race 11 times in KHI's No. 33 Chevrolet and three times in the No. 77 Chevrolet, with no truck or ARCA outings on his agenda.

    "Kevin and Delana (Harvick) want to put me in the same thing this year," Gale said. "Instead of having me jumping around from schedule to schedule, I'll drive the same type car, work with the same crew chief and get something a little more consistent as far as the schedule goes.

    "Kevin is doing most of the races. He's a two-time Busch Series champion, so he knows what it takes to get the car up to speed and he'll do all the little things that it takes to have a winning car. And that makes it that much better for me when I get in it. I'm really excited about it."

    KHI enters its fifth year of full-time NASCAR competition with a revamped lineup. The No. 2 Chevy truck team that Gale raced with last year has been turned over to a full-time driver, three-time series champion Jack Sprague. He'll join the truck series' other three-time champ, Ron Hornaday, who won the title again last season, in the KHI garage.

    Harvick won the Busch Series championship in 2001 and 2006 driving for the owner of his Cup car, Richard Childress. This year, Harvick is confining his Nationwide efforts to his car. He'll drive the No. 33 22 times. KHI's No. 77, which ran a full schedule last year, has become a part-time team for 2008, as Harvick has done away with the revolving roster of Cup stars, most notably Tony Stewart and Bobby Labonte, that has highlighted KHI's Busch efforts in the past.

    "I think we have a good group of guys," Gale said of the new No. 33 team. "With one full-time team this year, I think they took the best guys in the garage and put them together."

    Gale has 11 Busch outings over the past two seasons, with a best finish of 13th. Gale said this year he'd be in enough events to make a run at the rookie of the year award. Rusty Wallace Inc.'s Chase Austin, Hendrick Motorsports' Landon Cassill and Joe Gibbs Racing's Joey Logano are Gale's announced competition for Nationwide's first rookie award.

    "I had a whole winter to think about what went on last year -- going over my weak points and what I did right, too," the two-time Mobile International Speedway champion said. "There's a lot of little things you can work on. There's a lot of luck involved, but you have to be able to put a whole race together, and I was having a hard time putting a whole race together. You have to realize that when the race is halfway over, even if you're doing great, you've still got half a race to go. These races are 300 miles long; it's a lot different from running a 100-lapper at Mobile International.

    "Obviously, my goal (in 2008) is to go out there and win a few races. But I had lots of ups and downs last year. This being my second year with Kevin Harvick Inc., each and every week I want to work on my consistency. I want my car owners to be able to count on a consistently good finish. I want to get to where I can run in the top 10 consistently, then the top five. If you do that, then the wins will come."

    The next phase of Gale's career starts Sunday, with three days of testing at Daytona International Speedway. The Nationwide portion of NASCAR's Preseason Thunder starts on Friday with half the teams testing through Sunday morning, and Harvick will handle the No. 33 during those tests. On Sunday afternoon, the rest of the teams start their turn, with Gale piloting the No. 77.

    "On the first day, you work on getting the most speed you can out of the car," Gale said. "You want to get that car just as slick as you can to get it to go as fast as it can by itself. Then toward the end of the second day, you start to work on how your car runs in the draft."

    Gale was at Daytona last week, handling the shocks for KHI during truck testing. After Nationwide testing, he'll be back at Daytona on Feb. 16 in the No. 77 for the season-opening Camping World 300. His schedule resumes on March 8 when he'll tackle Atlanta Motor Speedway for the first time. Atlanta and Lowe's Motor Speedway in Charlotte, N.C., are the only venues on Gale's schedule where he hasn't raced previously.

    "I think that will be a big thing for me," Gale said of his familiarity with most of the tracks. "Last year, I went to a lot of tracks that I had never raced on before, and it's hard to learn a new track in a couple of practices. It seemed like once I'd get the hang of it, the race would be over."

    Gale will race the No. 33 for the first time this season at Nashville Superspeedway on March 22. His schedule includes a return to Alabama for the Aaron's 312 at Talladega Superspeedway on April 26. Gale raced in last year's truck event at Talladega. This time, he'll be in the No. 33 Chevy that finished second in the 2007 race when Stewart was edged by KHI teammate Labonte in a fantastic finish.
