Carle sparks A-League interest

By Liam FitzGibbon

Newcastle Jets would love to entice Nick Carle back to the A-League with Turkish club Genclerbirligi reportedly set to axe the Socceroos midfielder.

Carle joined the Superlig side from the Jets after taking out last season's A-League player of the year award, but has become unsettled despite initially impressing with some good performances.

Genclerbirligi, who finished fourth in the league last season, have slipped down the ladder and Carle, 26, is one of several players tipped to be shown the door during a host of changes in the January transfer window.

"Carle could not adapt to the team and our coach does not want him in the team," club chairman Ilhan Cacav said.

"We must follow our coach's wishes."

Carle's impending availability is likely to spark interest from several A-league clubs, none more-so than Newcastle, who have missed the playmaker's attacking flair in midfield this season.

Jets chief executive John Tsatsimas said while signing Carle this late in the season was out of the question, the club would "look at all avenues" for next season.

"Every A-League club would love to have Nicky Carle," Tsatsimas said.

"He's been a success at Newcastle, he's tried and tested with our club and our city so we'd love to have him, but obviously there are other factors involved.

"The majority of that involves dollars and cents.

"Having said that, if we could have Nicky Carle tomorrow we would certainly love to have him in the side, as would any Novocastrian."

Most A-League clubs would find it difficult to squeeze Carle under their salary cup, though the Jets could sign him as their marquee player for next season in place of Mario Jardel, whose A-League stint has been a failure.

But Tsatsimas said the biggest obstacle to bringing Carle back to the A-League could be the midfielder's desire to be a success in Europe.

"He's made a step abroad before and it didn't work out for him and I think he was a bit disappointed he didn't stay longer," Tsatsimas said.

"I think this time he's more of the opinion he'd stick it out a bit longer.

"I really don't think the A-League is an option at the moment for him - but that may change."

Carle had intended to use his overseas move to enhance his Socceroos prospects.

But with the squads of several of next year's World Cup qualifiers likely to be picked largely from the A-League, playing in Australia could now be a more attractive option.

Adelaide coach Aurelio Vidmar said his club would explore the possibility of chasing Carle's signature if he became available.

"We're always interested in quality players and he certainly is that," Vidmar said.

Both Adelaide and Melbourne could offer Carle the added incentive of Asian Champions League football, starting in March, and there are no salary cap restrictions for the tournament.

The Victory and Sydney FC said they had not thought about the prospect of signing Carle but would not discount it.

Meanwhile, Sydney chief executive George Perry said he knew nothing of reports veteran midfielder Ufuk Talay was set to join former coach Pierre Littbarski and teammates Mark Rudan and Alvin Ceccoli at Japanese second division club Avispa Fukuoka.

Perry said the club was tabling a new contract offer for the former Galatasaray player.
