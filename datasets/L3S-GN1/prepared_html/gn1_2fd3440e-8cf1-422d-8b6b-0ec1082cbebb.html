CDMA closure delayed at least three months

Article from:
  • Font size: Decrease Increase
  • Email article: Email
  • Print article: Print

Fleur Leyden

January 19, 2008 12:00am

TELSTRA'S planned CDMA closure has been delayed by at least three months and it has been told to give some customers free handsets for the new Next G network.

Communications Minister Stephen Conroy said yesterday that a report by the Australian Communications and Media Authority had shown the replacement Next G network still needed work.

He said Telstra's planned January 28 closure of the CDMA network would held off to at least April 28.

"ACMA found, and I agree, that the Next G footprint is equivalent to the CDMA network," he said.

"ACMA also found that the Next G coverage using an external aerial was equivalent; however issues were identified with handheld coverage, which I have asked Telstra to address."

Some Telstra customers, especially in rural and regional areas, report problems with the Next G network.

Senator Conroy's predecessor, Helen Coonan, last year slapped a licence condition on Telstra preventing it from switching off CDMA until it matched the coverage and performance of Next G.

Mr Conroy said yesterday that Telstra would report within a fortnight on how network problems could be fixed and would report on the outcome by April 28.

"Subject to me receiving a report that addresses my concerns . . . consumers should work on the basis that the CDMA network will be switched off," he said.

Telstra will be required to advertise how Next G customers can resolve their issues and free handsets will be given to those that show genuine coverage problems.

Telstra would not reveal how many customers remain on the CDMA network or how much it will cost to keep it running for another three months or more.

Telstra chief financial officer John Stanhope said the longer running time for CDMA would not affect the telco's last financial guidance.

Telstra's shares closed down 7 cents to $4.41 while its instalment receipts were off 8 cents at $2.84.
