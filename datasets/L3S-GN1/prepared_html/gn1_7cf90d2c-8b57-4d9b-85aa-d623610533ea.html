Austin leaves Roughriders for alma mater

Murray McCormick, Canwest News Service; Regina Leader-Post

Published:Â Thursday, January 17, 2008

REGINA - Kent Austin resigned as head coach of the Grey Cup-champion Saskatchewan Roughriders on Wednesday, choosing to accept the offensive co-ordinator's position with his alma mater, the University of Mississippi Rebels.

The 44-year-old Austin, who guided the Roughriders to Grey Cups as a quarterback in 1989 and head coach last season, made the announcement during a media conference at Mosaic Stadium and said he expects to start his new job Friday.

Austin and Houston Nutt, the head coach at Ole Miss, reached an agreement in principle late Tuesday. It was obvious from Austin's comments and reactions during the media conference that it was a decision he made after a great deal of consideration.

"I have a great love and affection for my university,'' said Austin, who attended Ole Miss from 1981-85 after growing up in Nashville, Tenn. "But it's not just the university. It's the people I'll be working with. That is very important. If it had not been for the quality individual of Houston Nutt and his staff, even four hours from home, I would not have gone."

Roughriders general manager Eric Tillman, who hired Austin on Dec. 6, 2006, backed up his good friend's thoughts. Tillman has stated since the news broke that Austin was in the running for the job that it was Austin's ties to Ole Miss that sealed the deal.

"This has been an emotional process for Kent and for us,'' said Tillman, who is also an alumnus of Ole Miss. "If this had been any other university, we wouldn't be having the dialogue. When Ole Miss called, because it's a special situation, we gave them permission to talk to Kent.

"We recognized that it was his alma mater, the proximity to home and the special relationship he had with the university. Kent and I met and he made the decision to go home.''

The ties of home were so strong that Austin turned down a contract extension that would have made him one of the highest paid coaches in the Canadian Football League. It's believed if Austin had been able to match his accomplishments of 2007 this season, he would have received $450,000 annually from the Roughriders.

"I don't make decisions based on money,'' said Austin. "The money is important, I get that, but if it was about the money I'd be staying here.''

Austin's contract with Ole Miss is still being worked out, but it's believed he'll earn $350,000.

The Roughriders will now embark on the search for a new head coach. Tillman said there isn't any time frame for hiring Austin's replacement.

"We will take as long as necessary to make the best decision that we can,'' Tillman said. "We look externally and internally. We have some quality people here who contributed to our success.''

Among the assistant coaches with the Roughriders who are possible candidates is defensive co-ordinator Richie Hall, assistant coach Mike Gibson and offensive co-ordinator Ken Miller. Other candidates are Calgary Stampeders offensive co-ordinator George Cortez and B.C. Lions defensive co-ordinator Dave Ritchie.

Ole Miss had been searching for an offensive co-ordinator since David Lee accepted a job with the NFL's Miami Dolphins. Lee was Austin's quarterbacks coach for his first two seasons at Ole Miss, and he encouraged new Rebels head coach Nutt to interview Austin.
