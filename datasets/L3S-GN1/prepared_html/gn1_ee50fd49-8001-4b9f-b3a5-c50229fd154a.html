Montreal closes out road swing with a trip to New York

(Sports Network) - The Montreal Canadiens close out their season-long six-game road trip tonight, as they visit the Big Apple and Madison Square Garden to tangle with the New York Rangers.

Montreal is 3-1-1 so far on the trip and 13-6-2 overall on the road. The Canadiens have won their last two games, including a 5-1 victory in Florida on Friday. Christopher Higgins had a goal and two assists and Cristobal Huet made 40 saves to lead Montreal.

Mark Streit added a goal and an assist for Montreal, while Guillaume Latendresse, Michael Ryder and Tom Kostopoulos also scored for the Canadiens, who have scored five goals in each of their last three wins.

Saku Koivu had an assist to give him a team-high 23 assists on the season.

Following tonight's game, the Canadiens open the 2008 calendar with a three- game homestand.

New York was in action on Saturday and exploded for a season-high six goals, five on the power play, in a 6-1 rout of Toronto. Jaromir Jagr registered a goal and three assists.

Scott Gomez had an assist to stretch his point streak to a career-high 11 games, the longest active streak in the NHL. After a slow start to the season, Gomez has four goals and 11 assists on his current run and a team-high 34 points (9 goals, 25 assists) on the season.

Jagr, meanwhile, has at least a point in six straight games, totaling three goals and eight assists over his burst.

Martin Straka added a goal and a helper for the Rangers, who have won two in a row and are three points back of first-place New Jersey in a tight Atlantic Division. Petr Prucha scored twice, while Blair Betts and Brendan Shanahan tallied once each.

Stephen Valiquette made 27 stops to post his second win of the season.

New York is 12-8-0 at home this year and follow this home tilt with three straight on the road.

The Rangers and Canadiens are meeting for the first time this season after New York took three of four in the series last year.
