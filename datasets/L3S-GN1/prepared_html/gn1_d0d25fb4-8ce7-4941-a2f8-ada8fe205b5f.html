Ministers to visit to flooded Qld north

  • Email
  • Print
  • Normal font
  • Large font
January 14, 2008 - 9:24PM

Queensland Treasurer and Acting Emergency Services Minister Andrew Fraser will visit flood-damaged north Queensland on Tuesday morning to inspect inundated homes, roads and infrastructure.

A spokesman for Mr Fraser said he would fly over flooded towns including Proserpine and the tourist centre of Airlie Beach in the Whitsundays where the Bruce Highway remains cut and heavy rain was expected to continue for at least another day.

After the inspection, he will speak to media in Mackay.

It was understood he may announce emergency relief measures, including state and federal national disaster relief and recovery arrangements.

Late on Monday night, an overloaded sewerage pumping station at Airlie Beach was struggling to cope with the storm water deluge, said Emergency Management Queensland (EMQ) spokesman John Hall.

Speaking from the city of Mackay, Mr Hall said five SES crew members were taken by helicopter from Mackay to Airlie Beach to help relieve tired crew who have battled to sandbag more than 60 homes and businesses.

Another seven volunteers from Mackay managed to reach the nearby town of Proserpine early on Monday afternoon, despite floodwaters cutting the Bruce Highway and other roads, Mr Hall said.

Airlie Beach still had electricity but the sewerage system was becoming a concern, he said.

"Airlie Beach remains cut off by road from Proserpine and there have been more reports of landslips at Airlie Beach," Mr Hall said.

"Also they are having problems with the sewer system now because the pumping station has been inundated.

"But at this stage, there is no threat to health," he said.

A woman suffering complications during childbirth also triggered a medivac emergency at Bowen Hospital.

"Normally, she would be taken by road ambulance to Proserpine Hospital," Mr Hall said.

"However, the road between Bowen and Proserpine is cut."

The EMQ helicopter was called in to airlift the woman to Townsville Hospital.

Queensland Rail said the Cairns Sunlander train was stranded in Proserpine at 2am (AEST) on Monday and around 200 passengers had to be taken back to Mackay and then flown to Townsville.

Those passengers met the Cairns Tilt Train in Townsville to carry them onto their destination, a QR spokeswoman said.

Meanwhile, the Brisbane-bound Sunlander due to leave Cairns at 8.35am (AEST) on TuesdaY will now depart from Mackay at 23.10 (AEST) on Monday.

The weather bureau has forecast more rain this week from a persistent monsoon low off Townsville.

In the 24 hours to 9am on Monday, 353mm of rain fell at Proserpine airport and 289mm fell at Giru, near Townsville and 157mm in Townsville itself.
