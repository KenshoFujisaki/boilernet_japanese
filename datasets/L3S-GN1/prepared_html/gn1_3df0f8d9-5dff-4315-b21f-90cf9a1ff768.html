Canadians satisfied with start

Ames, Weir third, fourth in season opener

CanWest News Service; with files from Reuters

Published:Â Tuesday, January 08, 2008

Canadians Stephen Ames and Mike Weir got a jump on the PGA money list by finishing third and fourth, respectively, at the season-opening Mercedes-Benz Championship in Kapalua, Hawaii on Sunday.

Ames earned $410,000 U.S. for his third-place finish and Weir $319,000 for ending up fourth.

Swede Daniel Chopra beat American Steve Stricker in a playoff, both tying at 18-under after regulation. Calgary resident Ames shot a final-round 66 Sunday to finish alone in third place at 17-under 275 in the event open exclusively to winners on the PGA Tour last year.

"I'm happy, considering I've had two months off coming into here and haven't had much practice," Ames told reporters after the final round. "And the way I've hit it and played, I'm quite content with the way I finished this week."

Weir, of Bright's Grove, Ont., struggled early Sunday, bogeying two of his first four holes, and shot a 70 to finish one shot behind Ames.

Chopra beat Stricker on the fourth playoff hole to earn his second career Tour title.

Both players missed birdie opportunities from around six feet at the first playoff hole before Chopra sealed the victory at the $1.1-million event with a two-putt birdie at the par-5 ninth, his eagle attempt having come to rest on the edge of the cup.

"It was unbelievable," a beaming Chopra told reporters after delivering a master class with his putter to book his place at the Masters for the first time.

"What a great round I played (Sunday). I kept making birdies and I tried to avoid looking up at the board. I looked a couple of times and I saw I had the lead, and then I looked up again and all of a sudden Stricker kept making them (birdies).

Chopra, who had narrowly missed a 13-footer to seal victory at the 72nd hole, fell agonizingly short with a 24-footer for birdie on the second extra hole.

"All those putts in the playoff and in regulation, I thought I hit superb strokes," he said. "Every single one of them I hit exactly the way I wanted and I felt like they were going in the hole, and then I thought maybe it wasn't meant to be.

"I'm awfully happy. Now I get to go to Augusta, my lifelong dream."

Weir said the only shot he wanted to have back was his first one on Sunday.

"Obviously hit a bad tee shot on 1," he told reporters. "Outside of that, I thought I played pretty well, just got nothing out of it.

"I thought the three-putt on 5 hurt, three-putted 5 and then hit a really good drive on 6 and it rolled all the way across into a bad lie where I had 60 yards and didn't have a shot. Yeah, I battled back, but it was a tough start."

Weir was the leader heading into the final round. He said he'll keep the entire weekend in perspective and won't focus on his struggles Sunday.

"I'm not going to beat myself up," he said. "I played well, just a bad start. If I could have made some putts, I could have easily still shot 5-, 6-, 7-under maybe and been right there."

Stricker shot a 9-under 64 to force the playoff, but was unable to finish off Chopra in the extra holes.

"This is definitely a good start for the season," he said.
