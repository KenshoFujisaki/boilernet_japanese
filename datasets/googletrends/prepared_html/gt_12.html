Everson Griffen involved in hotel incident over weekend

By Anthony Broome 7 hours ago

Everson Griffen did not suit up for the Minnesota Vikings on Sunday, which was expected as he was ruled out with a knee injury on Friday before the game. However, he was not present at U.S. Bank Stadium in Minneapolis in Week 3, which led to speculation about where the star defensive endÂ was.Â Head coach Mike Zimmer was asked about the situation after the loss to the Buffalo Bills and would only say that Griffen was not there due to a "personal matter" and that he was leaving it there.

Now, details have emerged about his absence after internet speculation ran wild on Monday. KSTP in the Twin Cities is reporting that the veteran lineman was involved in an incident at the Hotel Ivy in Minneapolis on Saturday where gun violence was threatened and Griffen had some sort of breakdown. Here is a snippet from their report, which states that Griffen was not arrested. The information here was gathered via police records:

"According to police records, a person at Hotel Ivy was verbally threatening to shoot someone in the middle of the day on Saturday.

The Minneapolis Police Department redacted that person's name from the report, but a source confirmed that it was indeed Griffen named in the report.

According to the report, at one point staff members moved to the back of the office while Griffen paced around in the lobby.

Police dispatch audio archived online includes a dispatcher saying that the suspect said to the staff that if someone wouldn't let him in his room that he was going to shoot someone. Police said no gun was ever seen."

Another report from FOX9 stated that Griffen was arrested for a separate incident after the hotel ordeal for trying to break into the home of a Vikings teammate. The Vikings denied FOX9's report, but local media still is reporting that he is in a local hospital being evaluated. The tweet from producer Seth Kaplan was eventually deleted.

KTSP also was able to grab a statement from general manager Rick Spielman, which says that the team is aware of the situation and is concerned about the well-being of one of their core players.

"We are aware of the situation involving Everson Griffen and certainly concerned by what we have heard. We are currently focused on Everson's well-being and providing the appropriate support for him and his family," Spielman said.

The 30-year-old Griffen signed a four-year contract worth $58 million total last preseason and is under contract with the Vikings through the 2022 season. He is a three-time Pro Bowler and has gathered 61 sacks in his eight seasons in the league thus far. He was a fourth-round pick out of the University of Southern California in the 2010 NFL Draft and has spent his entire career to this point with the Vikings.

10 COMMENTS

No word yet has been given on when Griffen could return to the team and there are still plenty of details to sort out. The Vikings are currently on a short week as they prepared for a trip out to the west coast to take on the Los Angeles Rams on Thursday Night Football this week. The team currently sits at 1-1-1 on the season and is tied with the Green Bay Packers for second place in the NFL North.
